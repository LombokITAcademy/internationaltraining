Grammar 16: Future Perfect Tense in English

The **Future Perfect Tense** is used to describe an action that will be completed before a certain point in the future. It is formed using **will have + past participle**. Example: "By next year, she **will have graduated** from university." / "They **will have finished** the project by tomorrow evening." The negative form is made using **will not (won’t) have + past participle**. Example: "He **won’t have completed** the book by then." Questions are formed using **Will + subject + have + past participle?** Example: "Will you have finished your work by 5 PM?"

Grammar 17: Future Perfect Continuous Tense in English

The **Future Perfect Continuous Tense** is used to describe an action that will have been happening for a period of time before a specific point in the future. It is formed using **will have been + verb-ing**. Example: "By next month, she **will have been working** at the company for five years." / "They **will have been traveling** for 10 hours by midnight." The negative form is made using **will not (won’t) have been + verb-ing**. Example: "He **won’t have been studying** long enough to take the test." Questions are formed using **Will + subject + have been + verb-ing?** Example: "Will you have been living in this city for 10 years by next year?"

Grammar 18: Simple Past Future Tense in English

The **Simple Past Future Tense** is used to describe an action that was intended or planned to happen in the future from a past perspective. It is commonly used in indirect speech, reported speech, or conditional sentences. It is formed using **would + base verb** or **was/were going to + base verb**. Example: "She said she **would visit** her grandmother the next day." / "They **were going to travel** to Paris, but they changed their minds." The negative form is made using **would not (wouldn’t) + base verb** or **was/were not going to + base verb**. Example: "He said he **wouldn’t buy** a new car." / "She **wasn’t going to attend** the meeting." Questions are formed using **Would + subject + base verb?** Example: "Would he call you later?" / "Was she going to join the event?"
