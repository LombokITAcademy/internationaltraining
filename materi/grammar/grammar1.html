Grammar 1: English Grammar Basics

Grammar is the set of rules that govern how we form sentences in a language. A **noun** is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. A **verb** shows an action or a state of being. **Adjectives** describe nouns, while **adverbs** modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. **Pronouns** replace nouns, and **prepositions** show relationships between words. Proper grammar helps us communicate clearly and effectively.

Grammar 2: Present Tense in English

The **Present Simple Tense** is used to describe habits, facts, and routines. We use the base form of the verb for "I", "you", "we", and "they". For "he", "she", and "it", we add **-s** or **-es** to the verb. Example: "She **likes** ice cream." / "They **go** to school every day." The **Present Continuous Tense** is used for actions happening now. It is formed with **am/is/are + verb-ing**. Example: "He **is reading** a book right now."

Grammar 3: Present Continuous Tense in English

The **Present Continuous Tense** is used to describe actions that are happening right now or temporary situations. It is formed using **am/is/are + verb-ing**. Example: "She **is reading** a book now." / "They **are playing** soccer in the park." It is also used for future planned events: "I **am traveling** to Japan next week." To form a negative sentence, we use **not**: "He **is not watching** TV." Questions are formed by placing **am/is/are** before the subject: "Are they studying now?"
