Grammar 7: Simple Past Tense in English

The **Simple Past Tense** is used to describe completed actions in the past. Regular verbs add **-ed** to form the past tense, while irregular verbs have different forms. Example: "She **visited** her grandmother last weekend." / "They **went** to the park yesterday." The negative form is made using **did not (didn't) + base verb**. Example: "He **did not eat** breakfast this morning." Questions are formed using **Did + subject + base verb?** Example: "Did she call you yesterday?"

Grammar 8: Past Continuous Tense in English

The **Past Continuous Tense** is used to describe actions that were happening at a specific time in the past. It is formed using **was/were + verb-ing**. Example: "She **was reading** a book at 8 PM." / "They **were playing** soccer when it started to rain." The **Past Continuous Tense** is often used with **Simple Past Tense** to show interruptions. Example: "I **was sleeping** when the phone **rang**." The negative form is made using **wasn't/weren't + verb-ing**. Example: "He **wasn't watching** TV at that time."

Grammar 9: Past Perfect Tense in English

The **Past Perfect Tense** is used to describe an action that happened **before another past action**. It is formed using **had + past participle**. Example: "She **had finished** her work before I arrived." / "They **had left** when we got there." The **Past Perfect Tense** is often used with **Simple Past Tense** to show which action happened first. Example: "I **had eaten** dinner before they called." The negative form is made using **had not (hadn't) + past participle**. Example: "He **hadn't seen** that movie before last night."
